How We Train

The system behind every session

Six principles govern everything on the floor — from the first warm-up rep to the heaviest bar of the cycle.

01

Power First, Always

Jumps and throws open every session — fresh, full rest, high intent. The variation rotates weekly so intent stays high, and no movement repeats two weeks in a row.

02

Progress On Proof

Add load only when all reps are clean and bar speed holds for two straight sessions. That rule governs main lifts and accessories alike.

03

Strength Without Grinders

Heavy work is capped by percentage or RPE so technique stays clean and recovery stays predictable. No maxing out through ugly reps.

04

Adapt After Fatigue, Not Through It

Three true deloads — Weeks 7, 11, and 15 — where the bars actually come down. No loading block ever exceeds four consecutive weeks.

05

Test What We Train

Vertical, broad jump, Fly 10, 5-10-5, bench, front squat, and the mile — same surface, same warm-up, same timing method, every time.

06

Availability Beats Load

Rows and pull-ups protect shoulders; single-leg and Copenhagen work keeps hamstrings and groins available. The best ability is availability.
