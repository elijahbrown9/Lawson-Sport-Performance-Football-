# Weight Training Course Blueprint

This document helps you design and run a complete weight training class (e.g., semester course, 8–12 week block, or offseason development cycle).

## 1) Define Course Basics

- **Course name:** (Example: Foundations of Strength Training)
- **Audience:** (Example: high school athletes, beginners, mixed experience)
- **Length:** (Example: 12 weeks)
- **Schedule:** (Example: 3 sessions/week, 50–60 min each)
- **Primary goals:**
  - Improve movement quality and lifting technique
  - Build strength and muscular endurance
  - Teach safe training habits and independence
- **Available equipment:** racks, barbells, dumbbells, kettlebells, benches, bands, machines, open floor space

## 2) Learning Outcomes (What Students Should Be Able to Do)

By the end of the course, students should be able to:

1. Demonstrate safe setup and spotting procedures.
2. Perform key movement patterns with proper form:
   - Squat
   - Hinge
   - Push
   - Pull
   - Brace/carry
3. Explain basic training concepts:
   - Sets, reps, rest, intensity, progressive overload
4. Build and complete a structured workout plan.
5. Track and reflect on performance improvements.

## 3) Suggested Course Units

## Unit 1 (Weeks 1–2): Safety + Movement Foundations

- Weight room expectations, etiquette, and spotting
- Dynamic warm-up routine
- Bodyweight pattern assessment
- Intro to RPE (effort scale) and training logs

## Unit 2 (Weeks 3–5): Technique Development

- Teach and coach primary lifts (goblet/front/back squat variants, RDL/deadlift pattern, press/row)
- Introduce tempo, control, and full range of motion
- Light-to-moderate loading with high coaching feedback

## Unit 3 (Weeks 6–9): Progressive Overload

- Increase load gradually while maintaining form
- Rotate accessory work for balance and injury prevention
- Introduce basic conditioning finishers and recovery habits

## Unit 4 (Weeks 10–12): Application + Assessment

- Students run portions of their own training sessions
- Final movement competency check
- Personal progress presentation/reflection

## 4) Session Template (50–60 Minutes)

1. **Attendance + readiness check** (2–3 min)
2. **Warm-up + mobility activation** (8–10 min)
3. **Technique block / primary lift** (15–20 min)
4. **Secondary lift + accessories** (15–20 min)
5. **Conditioning/core/carry finisher** (5–8 min)
6. **Cool down + logbook entry** (3–5 min)

## 5) Example Weekly Split (3 Days)

- **Day A (Lower + Push emphasis)**
  - Squat variation
  - Bench or DB press
  - Split squat/lunge
  - Core anti-extension
- **Day B (Hinge + Pull emphasis)**
  - Hinge/deadlift variation
  - Row/pull-up progression
  - Posterior chain accessory
  - Carry variation
- **Day C (Total body + power mechanics)**
  - Jumps/med-ball throws (if appropriate)
  - Full-body strength circuit
  - Conditioning interval

## 6) Assessment Plan

Use a mix of performance, process, and knowledge:

- **Technique Rubrics (40%)**
  - Setup, control, depth/range, breathing/bracing, spotting
- **Training Log + Consistency (25%)**
  - Attendance, completion, reflection notes
- **Fitness/Performance Markers (20%)**
  - Baseline vs final (e.g., push-ups, plank time, estimated strength improvements)
- **Quiz/Reflection (15%)**
  - Safety rules, exercise selection logic, personal goal review

## 7) Progression Guidelines

- Add load only if technique quality is consistent.
- Typical progression options:
  - +2.5 to +10 lbs depending on exercise and athlete readiness
  - Add 1–2 reps per set before increasing load
  - Add sets for volume during mid-block weeks
- Deload or reduce load when signs of fatigue or poor movement appear.

## 8) Safety and Classroom Management Checklist

- Clear lifting station assignments
- Required spotter procedures for key lifts
- “No rep” standards for unsafe technique
- Hydration and footwear policy
- Emergency action plan and injury reporting process

## 9) What to Prepare Before Launch

- Parent/admin course overview
- Student pre-participation questionnaire
- Baseline testing day plan
- Technique rubrics and printable training log sheets
- Equipment map and rotation plan

## 10) First Week Action Plan

1. Day 1: Course expectations, safety briefing, movement screen
2. Day 2: Teach warm-up and squat/push patterns
3. Day 3: Teach hinge/pull patterns, start logs
