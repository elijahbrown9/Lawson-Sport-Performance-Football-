Master Thread

Purpose & design principles

Weekly Structure

Day 1 Hinge + Reactive · Day 2 Bench + Row Strength · Day 3 Front Squat + Single Leg + Posterior · Day 4 Incline + Row Cluster + Mobility. Speed work runs on its own 4-day track.

Power First

Jumps and throws are trained fresh, with full resets and high intent — always before the heavy bars.

Strength Without Grinders

Heavy work is capped by percentage or RPE so technique stays clean and recovery stays predictable.

No Movement Repeats In A Week

Jump variations rotate weekly; the split-squat pattern appears once per week (RFESS only).

Throughput

Sessions are built around a rack-dominant room and fast transitions so 50+ athletes can move through cleanly.

Adapt After Fatigue, Not Through It

Three true deloads (Weeks 7, 11, 15). Loading blocks never exceed four consecutive weeks.

KPI System

Testing rhythm

Test what we train, train what we test. Standardization: same surface, same warm-up, same timing method, same number of attempts. Record the best and the full attempt series.

Athlete Tracks

The two-track system

One template, two intensities. Assign each athlete a track at baseline using the testing data; promote at any KPI week. A 12" jumper and a 34" jumper should not do the same depth drop.

FND · Foundation Track

Assignment: vertical < 22" OR front squat < 1.0x bodyweight OR first off-season.

Plyos stay extensive: low box jumps, snap downs, hurdle hops on grass — no depth drops. Main lifts hold the bottom of every %/RPE range; add reps before load. Technique standard beats load standard every time.

VAR · Varsity Track

Assignment: vertical ≥ 22" AND front squat ≥ 1.0x bodyweight AND clean landings.

Full plyo menu including depth drops and contrast work as written. Main lifts work the top of the %/RPE range when bar speed is good. Promotion is earned at KPI weeks — never mid-block.

Nobody loses the front squat to wrist mobility

Daily micro-dose in every warm-up: 2 minutes — wrist rocks, banded lat stretch, elbow-punch front-rack hold with an empty bar. Small dose, every day, beats a mobility day nobody does.

Speed & Conditioning

Speed development · 4-day track

Runs alongside the lifting week. Full recoveries on all sprint work — speed is a skill, not conditioning. All distances in yards; total weekly high-intensity sprint volume stays under ~300yd.

Neck & Traps · Non-negotiable

Two exposures per week, ~5 minutes, built into lifting Days 2 and 4: band or partner iso holds (flexion / extension / lateral, 2x10s each) plus heavy DB shrugs 2x10. Cheap insurance for a collision sport.

Warm-Up Progression Map

WU1 → WU4

Cycle: WU1 → WU2 → WU3 → WU4, then repeat. As intensity rises, WU3 appears more often, then WU4 for deload and testing weeks. Block letters (A–F) in the session tables match the on-floor coaching language.

WU1

Teach + Groove

Positions, landing quality, hinge and squat patterning, low fatigue. Includes the 2-min front-rack micro-dose.

WU2

Build Speed

More stiffness and faster contacts; slightly higher intent on jumps and throws.

WU3

Fast + Elastic + Specific

Full intent on explosive work, longer rest, minimal fatigue.

WU4

Deload / KPI Prep

Fast but low volume; reduce soreness drivers; keep the nervous system sharp.
