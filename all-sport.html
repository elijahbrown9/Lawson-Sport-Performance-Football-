Program Overview

Purpose & how to run it

Weekly Structure

Day 1: Med ball power → Bench → Row → Posterior → Secondary press → Circuit. Day 2: Jump power → Front squat → Trap bar → Pull-Up Ladder → Single leg → Mobility circuit.

Power First, Always

Jumps and throws open every session — fresh, full rest, high intent. The variation rotates weekly so intent stays high.

Progress On Proof

Add load only when all reps are clean and bar speed holds for two straight sessions — that rule governs main lifts and accessories alike.

Adapt After Fatigue

Three true deloads (Weeks 7, 11, 15) where the bars actually come down — not just the circuits. No loading block exceeds four weeks.

Why These Movements

Bench for contact durability; front squat for posture and knee/hip mechanics; trap bar for athlete-friendly hinge strength; rows and pull-ups to protect shoulders; single-leg and Copenhagen work to keep hamstrings and groins available.

In-Season Rules

Game week: cap every lift at RPE 7, drop the last set of B and C blocks, keep the jumps and throws. No lifting at RPE 7+ the day before competition. Availability beats load.

Power rotation — no movement repeats two weeks in a row

Day 1 · Med Ball: Chest Pass → Overhead Slam → Rotational Throw → Split-Stance Slam. Day 2 · Jump: Low Box Jump → Broad Jump → Hurdle Hop → Lateral Bound. A daily 2-minute front-rack micro-dose lives inside every warm-up: wrist rocks, banded lat stretch, elbow-punch hold with an empty bar.
